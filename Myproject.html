The world's largest athletic apparel company, Nike is best known for its footwear, apparel, and equipment. Founded in 1964 as Blue Ribbon Sports, the company became Nike in 1971 after the Greek goddess of victory. One of the most valuable brands among sport businesses, Nike employs over 76,000 people worldwide.

However, there is one main ingredient in Nike's messaging: to “bring inspiration and innovation to every athlete.” Nike encourages anyone with a body, able or disabled, to maintain sports and an active lifestyle. The brand slogan says, “if you have a body, you're an athlete.”

NIKE, Inc. designs, develops, markets and sells high quality footwear, apparel, and equipment, accessories and services. Its athletic footwear products are designed primarily for specific athletic use, although a large percentage of the products are worn for casual or leisure purposes. It focuses on NIKE Brand and Brand Jordan product offerings in seven key categories: running, basketball, football, men’s training, women’s training, NIKE sportswear, and action sports. It also markets product designed for kids, as well as for other athletic and recreational uses such as baseball, cricket, golf, lacrosse, outdoor activities, football, tennis, volleyball, walking, and wrestling.

Nike's widespread popularity is a result of its unwavering commitment to quality and innovation, exemplified through cutting-edge technologies enhancing performance. Bolstered by its iconic "Swoosh" logo and association with the victorious Greek goddess, Nike's brand identity exudes athleticism and triumph. Astute marketing campaigns, including the iconic "Just Do It," resonate on emotional levels and feature renowned athletes, fostering personal connections. Partnering with influential sports figures like Michael Jordan and LeBron James, Nike leverages their success to bolster its own image. With a comprehensive product range spanning various sports and activities, coupled with a global distribution network, Nike garners universal recognition. Moreover, its engagement with social issues, collaborations, and adept use of digital platforms cements its status beyond sports apparel, establishing a cultural touchstone.

Nike, a renowned multinational corporation, is synonymous with athletic footwear, apparel, equipment, and accessories. Its name draws inspiration from the Greek goddess of victory, Nike, symbolizing triumph and prowess. This alignment with victory and strength mirrors the company's core focus on sports and competition. With its iconic "Swoosh" logo, Nike has solidified its position as a global leader in supplying athletic shoes and apparel, embodying the spirit of achievement and excellence that the goddess Nike personified in Greek mythology.
